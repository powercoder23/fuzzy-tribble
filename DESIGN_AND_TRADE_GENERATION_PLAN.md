# Design & Trade-Generation Plan — Paper Mode

*Prepared 2026-07-11. Scope: full wiring audit + how to reliably generate good paper trades + the V1→V2 bridge. No live orders. This synthesizes what is actually in the code today (verified by reading it), not what the docs claim.*

---

## 0. TL;DR

Your paper pipeline **already works and is wired end-to-end**. The discount scanner finds signals every 15 min, five gates filter them, `paper_trader` books them into `paper_trades.db`, a separate monitor thread re-prices and exits every 5 min, and Break-and-Bounce feeds the same book. The Convex V2 engine runs **observe-only** and its bridge switch (`GATE_SOURCE=engine`) already exists.

So this is **not** a "build it" job — it's a "make it reliable, make the trades good, and stage the V2 bridge" job. Priorities:

1. **P0 — Reliability & risk** (things that silently distort your paper results): the B&B phantom-open bug, the stale Sonar veto, no daily-loss guard, and the costs-skip-on-lot=1 hole. Until these are fixed, the paper P&L you'd use to green-light anything is generated by a system you'd never actually run.
2. **P1 — Trade quality & throughput**: vol-scaled exits (the single highest-leverage change), per-strategy cap semantics, MAE/MFE capture, and the "why am I seeing few/no trades" diagnostics.
3. **P2 — Bridge V1→V2 + cleanup**: run the engine gate in **soft** mode now, fix the two blockers the Week-1 review found (inverted grade ladder, one-legged regime), then flip to **hard**; split the `discount.py` god-module and add a `CONFIG.md`.

| `MIN_DTE_DAYS` | 5 | Skips near-expiry theta traps — **can dry up ideas near monthly expiry** |

## 2. Wiring health audit

### What is genuinely solid (keep, don't touch)
- **Single-writer SQLite + WAL + `busy_timeout` via `iv_store.connect()`** — the correct multi-container pattern. Only `iv-collector` writes `iv_history`; each scanner owns its `*_history` table.
- **Zero-API scanners** — all the `*_scanner.py` read `iv_history.db` only; no rate-limit contention with the collector.
- **OrderManager as the single lifecycle owner**, with B&B routed through `submit_external_signal` — this is a clean strangler seam toward V2.
- **Fail-open gates with a once-per-day loud Telegram alert** (`_alert_gate_failure`) — right trade-off for paper: a broken shared DB degrades to "trade unfiltered" but tells you.
- **Honest paper economics** — gap-aware stop fills, spread slippage, full NSE fee schedule, and a `factors_json` snapshot per trade for later edge attribution. Most retail systems never do this.
- **Separate monitor thread** so a multi-minute scan can never delay an SL or the square-off.
- **The V2 bridge switch already exists**: `entry_gate.GATE_SOURCE="engine"` reads `engine_decisions` and maps grades onto the composite shape.

### What is broken or risky (fix these — they distort paper results)

| Ref | Issue | Why it matters | Effort |
|-----|-------|----------------|--------|
| **BUG-1** | `paper_trader.apply_tick`: B&B books with `t1==t2, frac=1.0`. On target hit it books 100% at T1, sets `t1_done`, but **never finalizes** — Phase 2 is skipped because `qty_frac==0`. | Every B&B trade becomes a **phantom zero-qty open** until 15:20 → holds a Gate-5 slot + concentration cap (blocks real entries all afternoon), burns one chain fetch per 5-min tick, sends the wrong alert. Verified by execution in the 07-09 review. | ~5 lines + test |
| **BUG-2** | `process_signals` sonar veto calls `get_latest_sonar()` with **no same-day check** (returns the latest row *ever*). | At 09:30 yesterday's 15:25 `FLAT`/`BREAKDOWN` vetoes today's entries until sonar writes fresh rows; if the sonar container is down, **every** veto all day is stale. `OrderManager._check_position_risks` already filters `timestamp[:10]==today` — the entry veto must too. | ~5 lines |
| **RISK-1** | **No book-level daily-loss guard.** Per-trade rupee cap and count caps exist, but nothing stops 5 trades → 5 full stop-losses every day. B&B's `daily_loss_limit_pct` is dead code in paper mode. | This is the #1 risk control a trader wants first. Its absence means your paper track record has a risk profile you'd never run live. | small |
| **INTEG-1** | `_finalize` gates the fee model on `lot>1`. If the lot sizer fails, `main.py` falls back to `lambda _s: 1`, so **every trade that day books zero costs + zero slippage** — inflating P&L exactly when infra is degraded. | Silent paper-P&L inflation. Use an explicit sentinel (`lot_size=None`) instead of overloading `1`. | small |
| **CAP-1** | `book.count_today()` counts **all** rows, so every B&B booking eats the discount's `max_signals_per_day` budget while B&B ignores that cap. | On a busy breakout day B&B can starve the discount strategy (or vice-versa). Decide: per-strategy tag counting, or rename to an explicit global cap. | decision + small |

### Watch items (known, lower urgency)
- `discount.py` is ~3,170 lines (the god module) and is imported by every service — which is why even `sonar` needs the fat Docker image.
- **Config sprawl**: 12+ `*_config.py` + env vars + the settings-DB flag registry, three override layers deep (`settings_store` → env → config). It works, but precedence lives only in docstrings. One `CONFIG.md` table would prevent a foot-gun.
- `NSE_HOLIDAYS = []` never populated → DTE/expiry filters are off-by-one around every exchange holiday.
- **`paper_trades.db` read as "malformed" through my sandbox mount** (file header was valid and size kept changing between reads → almost certainly the lazy-sync mount, not real corruption). **Action:** run `PRAGMA integrity_check;` locally and keep the `data/paper_trades_backup_*.db` cadence. If it *is* corrupt, restore from backup and rebuild the WAL.

## 3. "How do I generate (more/better) trades?" — the practical answer

You already generate them; the questions are **reliability** ("why did I see zero today?") and **quality** ("were they worth taking?").

### 3a. Diagnostics — when you see too few or no trades, check in this order
1. **Is `iv-collector` alive and fresh?** Every gate silently depends on `iv_history` freshness. Surface the last-snapshot age as a global banner (the health endpoint already computes it). Stale data → scanners return nothing → zero trades and no obvious error.
2. **Near monthly expiry?** `MIN_DTE_DAYS=5` + "nearest-expiry only" means the universe dries up in the last ~5 days before a monthly. Expected, but it looks like a bug.
3. **Gate rejections** — today they exist only in logs. Build the **entry-funnel panel** (§5): `scanned → dropped by pre_market / breadth / entry / concentration / sonar veto / risk cap → booked`. This is the single most useful thing for answering "where did my trades go?"
4. **Stale sonar veto (BUG-2)** — a down sonar container silently vetoes everything.
5. **Phantom B&B opens (BUG-1)** — fills the 5 slots by mid-morning, blocking the afternoon.
6. **Token expiry** — check `data/tokens/upstox_access_token.json`; an expired token stops the collector and everything downstream.

### 3b. Quality — the highest-leverage change
**Vol-scale the exits.** Today SL −15% / T1 +25% / T2 +45% of premium is applied identically to a 0.15-delta 20-DTE option and a 0.40-delta 6-DTE option. Premium noise alone (2% spread fallback) trips a 15% stop on low-priced contracts. You already capture IV, delta, and expected-move per candidate at entry (in `factors_json`), so you can:
- anchor the SL to an **underlying** level or an ATR/expected-move band instead of a flat premium %, **or**
- scale the premium % by delta/DTE.

This is the one change most likely to move paper win-rate/expectancy — and because `factors_json` is captured per trade, you can **back-test it on your own history** before shipping. This depends on first capturing **MAE/MFE** (running max/min of `last_price` in `monitor()`, two columns) so you can see whether −15%/+25% are even the right levels.

### 3c. Throughput levers (only after reliability is fixed)
- Fix CAP-1 so B&B and discount don't starve each other; consider per-strategy daily caps (e.g. 3 discount + 3 B&B) instead of a shared 5.
- If you want more discount ideas, the safe knobs are `LIQUID_UNIVERSE_SIZE` (120→150) and the scan floor (`min_discount_score=45`), **not** loosening liquidity (`ALLOW_LOOSE_LIQUIDITY` admits near-untradeable strikes).
- A lighter post-14:00 cadence: entries stop at 15:00 but full-universe sweeps (with chain fetches) run to 15:15 only to feed the suggester — trim that to save API budget.

## 4. The V1 → V2 bridge (your chosen direction)

**Decision: keep V1 generating trades now; wire the engine's conviction in as a gate, staged.** The seam already exists — `entry_gate.GATE_SOURCE` and `_lookup_engine()` read `engine_decisions`. Do **not** retire `composite_scanner` / `trade_suggester` / `morning_confluence` yet — the Week-1 review is a NO-GO for full P1.

**Two blockers must be root-caused before the engine is trusted as the sole gate** (from `engine/WEEK1_REVIEW_2026-07-09.md`):
- **Inverted conviction ladder** — over its only (flat, VIX 11–12) week, higher grade → *worse* forward move (A+ was systematically the worst). Likely a flipped factor sign, a mislabeled trigger direction, or a score-weight problem. Audit factor signs / trigger labels / weights and **bump `FORMULA_VER`**.
- **Regime running one-legged** — `index_slope_pct` is NULL on all regime rows; the NIFTY SuperSmoother slope is never passed into `regime.load()`. Since lean drives the #1 reject (`with_the_tape`) and the CE/PE tilt, this shapes everything the engine emits. Wire the slope in.

**Staged rollout:**

| Stage | Action | Guard |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **B0 (now)** | Run `GATE_SOURCE=engine` **`GATE_MODE=soft`** alongside composite. Engine annotates/ranks, never blocks. Log both scores side-by-side per candidate. | Zero behaviour change; pure A/B data collection. |
| **B1** | Fix `index_slope_pct`; root-cause the inverted ladder; bump `FORMULA_VER`. | Re-run Week-1 style forward-edge check. |
| **B2** | Re-evaluate over **≥1 non-flat week** (some AMBER/RED days, higher VIX). Confirm grade→edge is monotone the *right* way. | Don't skip; the ladder was only ever measured on 4 flat days. |
| **B3** | Flip discount's `entry_gate` to `GATE_SOURCE=engine`, `GATE_MODE=hard`. Retire `composite_scanner` / `trade_suggester` / `morning_confluence` from the compose file. | Keep the V1 gates importable for one release as a rollback. |
| **B4** | Feed the engine live `risk_state` (`open_positions`, `day_pnl_pct`, `sl_hits_today`) from the paper book so its `slots_free` / `daily_loss_ok` / `sl_hits_ok` gates stop reading an empty dict. | This is also where RISK-1 (§2) and the engine's risk gates converge on one source of truth. |

Net: the engine becomes the **conviction + risk brain**; V1's `paper_trader`/`OrderManager` stays the **execution/lifecycle backend**. That's the clean end-state for "bridge both."

## 5. Dashboard — the cockpit gaps that block daily use

Present and good already: KPI strip, cumulative P&L, per-strategy performance, opportunities with honest staleness, IV/skew/decay analytics, convex journal + regime cockpit, settings + container control. Missing, in priority order:

1. **Open-positions panel** with live unrealized P&L, distance-to-SL/T1 per trade, and aggregate open-risk. Data is already in `paper_trades` (`last_price` updates every 5 min) — one endpoint away.
2. **Daily-loss meter** — today's realized + marked P&L vs the RISK-1 cap. The number a trader checks 20×/day.
3. **Entry-funnel / rejection panel** for the money path (§3a.3) — mirror what the convex journal already does for the engine.
4. **MAE/MFE chart** — enables the §3b exit tuning from real data.
5. **Cap/limit strip** — trades used (n/cap), per-symbol caps hit, direction/sector concentration state.
6. **Data-freshness banner** — global `iv_history` snapshot age (every gate depends on it silently).

## 7. Suggested sequencing

- **Sprint 1 (reliability + integrity):** items 1, 2, 4 — small, self-contained, each with a unit test. After this your paper P&L is trustworthy.
- **Sprint 2 (risk + visibility):** items 3, 5, 8 — the daily-loss guard plus the dashboard panels that make the guard and the funnel visible.
- **Sprint 3 (quality):** items 6, 7, 14 — vol-scaled exits validated on the backtest harness; fix cap semantics.
- **Sprint 4 (bridge):** items 9 → 10 → (Week-1-style re-eval) → hard flip; then 11, 12, 13 as cleanup lands.

Every change should ship with a unit test (you already have a strong `test_*.py` suite — extend it), and the two bridge stages should each be re-validated with a forward-edge check like the Week-1 review before advancing.
